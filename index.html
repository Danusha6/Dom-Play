Story Summarized

The story of one man’s search for meaning in life and his encounter with a pair of bumbling, not-so-bright church custodians. The pair are comparable to ‘Dumb & Dumber’, but they take the job of caring for the house of the Lord very seriously, as well as the task of “leaving the light on” for the lost who may pass by .

Kristy: I’m so excited, Emily! I feel like I know him already, you’ve told me so much about him.

Emliy: You’ll just love him - I know you will. He’s... he’s... wonderful.

How this page works

The webpage is a provide a script for "Leave A Light On" story.The webpage includes various elements such as a header with a title and a navigation menu. The main content consists of a summarized version of the story and a dialogue between characters. The dialogue portrays a conversation between three friends discussing the protagonist's upcoming marriage. They playfully speculate about potential flaws or quirks in the groom-to-be, highlighting their camaraderie and teasing nature. The dialogue ends with a hint that the story is to be continued. Additionally, the webpage incorporates a JavaScript page called "DOM Play." This script enhances the user's interaction with the page. By clicking on specific lines spoken by an actor, the app highlights those lines by changing their background color to orange. Simultaneously, lines spoken by other actors revert to their original white background color. This dynamic highlighting feature adds an engaging element to the storytelling experience. In more detailed way:-
- The highlight(el) function is defined, which toggles the background color of the clicked element between white and orange.
- The highlightActor(actor) function is defined, which receives the name of an actor as an argument.
- The spans variable is assigned all the "span" elements within the element with the id "play".
- A loop is set up to iterate over each span element.
